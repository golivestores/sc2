Frequently asked questions

Who is Good Life Meds?

‍At Good Life Meds, we’re committed to your well-being. As a 100% online process, we connect patients across all 50 states with licensed healthcare professionals and pharmacies to bring healthcare home. Whether you need support for sexual health, daily wellness, weight loss solutions, or hair care, our simple online process allows you to connect with medical experts licensed in your state. They can recommend prescription options and have them conveniently delivered right to your door. Your health, your convenience—Good Life Meds.

Our leading slogan encapsulates our mission: “Simplifying the path to the Good Life; Making happiness and healthiness easier to achieve.” We’re dedicated to bringing quality care home, and more affordable for everyone, all while maintaining the convenience of being all online.

What happens if I'm not approved for the medication?

If you are not prescribed the desired medication from our team of medical providers, you will be notified and receive a full refund for all cost incurred. This includes a cancellation of any subscription memberships you selected.

How long does it take to receive my medication?

Once your medical intake and health history form is reviewed and approved, your medication will be delivered to you within 3-5 business days. You can expect to receive your personalized medication in a timely manner.

Does Good Life require insurance?

Nope! Insurance isn’t needed at Good Life. We keep things simple with transparent pricing — your medication, medical visit, and shipping are all included in one affordable cost.

Do all purchases include prescriptions and medications?

Yes, Good Life purchases cover the cost of your doctor’s consult, prescription medications, and free shipping. There are no hidden or additional fees. Prescription medications are provided only after consultation with a licensed healthcare provider to ensure they are appropriate for you.Note: Compounded medications are prepared based on specific patient needs or in response to drug shortages and have not been reviewed or approved by the FDA for safety or effectiveness. Prescription required.

Are Good Life pharmacies FDA-regulated?

All formulations undergo third-party analytical testing to confirm potency and strength as labeled. Our pharmacies operate under strict regulatory guidelines. 503A pharmacies are licensed by state boards of pharmacy, while 503B pharmacies are registered and overseen by the FDA. Both types of pharmacies are subject to FDA regulations, ensuring compliance with industry standards for safety and quality.
